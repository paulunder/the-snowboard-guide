Beginner

What should you buy as a beginner?

First of all it is important, that you are sure you wanna start a new sport, which needs a lot effort in the beginning. If you are not sure, go and rent your gear and try it. After some renting sessions you can be sure you wanna do this and can buy gear without headache.

BOOTS

Snowboard Boots have some different values that are important for you as a beginner.

BOOT-Size

Your shoe size should fit, if your toes hit a little bit the front of the boot, then it is ok.

If it feels a little bit loose, then consider another shoe size or even another lacing system - f.e. BOA.

BOOT-Flex

The Flex defines how “hard” is your boot. Usually there are 3 Flex classes:

- SOFT (Flex 1-4)
- MID (Flex 4-7)
- HARD (Flex 7-10)

So for beginners most of the time the mid class is recommended, but you should also mention, what you wanna ride after you can handle the snowboard a little bit better.

SNOWBOARD

Essential is also the Snowboard, here it is even more critical to buy or rent the correct gear to learn as fast as possible.

There are 3 important shape-types for Snowboard Beginners

  1. Rocker
  2. Camber
  3. Flat

GEAR GURU's Recommendation for Beginners

Hybrid


Maybe you already thought that you can combine all these 3 types - this would also be suggestion for you as a beginner - I would highly recommend to get a 60% Camber and 40% Rocker. With such a board you can easily learn snowboarding and have also some fun while riding when you are more experienced.

This is called All-Mountain Board.

ROCKER

The Rocker is known for best times in deep snow. So if you are or wanna be a free rider, then this is your choice.
For Beginners this type of Snowboards is recommended, because you can do some faults while riding but you won't fall as easily as you would do with a Camber.

Except I have to say a pure Rocker is rarely seen, because it is not easy to handle a Rocker on the slope when you are more experienced and ride fast, that is because you cannot get so much pressure on the edges of the snowboard.

FLAT

The Flat Snowboard is the rarest Construction method of snowboards - it is recommended if you prefer make tricks in the park and hit some rails.

But the truth is that most of park snowboarders use Rocker or Cambers. So after all I would recommend not to buy it for your beginning sessions on the snowboard.

CAMBER

The Camber construction method is the most popular one. Because of the construction method here you have more pressure on your edges of your snowboard. This board is perfect for slopes, carving and advanced riders.

But because you have more pressure on you edges a small fault while snowboarding can lead to an accident and even injuries.
After all I just recommend Camber for beginners, when they are willing to fall more often, I always say if you prefer to ride on slopes or you like carving with your snowboard then a pure Camber is for you. If you are a beginner and want to do this stuff after learning snowboarding then maybe this is your choice.
